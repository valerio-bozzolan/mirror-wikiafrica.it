More about What is Wikipedia?

Wikipedia is a free online multilingual encyclopedia that anyone can use, contribute to or edit. It was launched in 2001. It offers millions of existing articles in over 240 languages that have all been written by people worldwide, just like you, who have an interest in a subject or want to share their knowledge and expertise with the world. The website and this large network of volunteers is supported by the non-profit Wikimedia Foundation.

 Every day, hundreds of thousands of visitors from around the world collectively edit or create entire articles to augment the knowledge held by the Wikipedia encyclopedia.Contributing volunteers decide what topic is covered and to what extent. This encourages and invites the contributing community to collaborate and share their knowledge with the world.

The extensive use of the site occurs within and outside the academic community. Considered to be a primary research tool, college students, faculty and researchers increasingly use the site as a tertiary source of information.

Articles can be added or edited by people of all ages, backgrounds and cultures. The contribution is more important than the expertise or qualifications of the contributor. Contributions cannot cause any harm to Wikipedia as the software allows easy reversal of mistakes. In addition, many experienced editors are watching to help ensure that edits are cumulative improvements. Begin by simply clicking the Edit link at the top of any editable page!

It is important to note that there are certain things that Wikipedia is not. Wikipedia is not a/an :

Paper encyclopedia; Dictionary; Publisher of original thought; Soapbox or means of promotion; Mirror or a repository of links, images, or media files; Blog, Web hosting service, social networking service, or memorial site; Directory; Manual, guidebook, textbook, or scientific journal; Crystal ball; Newspaper; Indiscriminate collection of information; Censored;Anarchy;Democracy; Bureaucracy; Battleground; Compulsory

FAQs

Who owns Wikipedia?
Wikipedia's technical framework is supported by a non-profit parent organization, the Wikimedia Foundation, which also supports Wikipedia's sister projects. The site is run by the community of Wikipedians guided by the principles articulated by Jimmy Wales, including the adherence to a neutral point of view.
How big is Wikipedia?
In the English version alone, Wikipedia currently has 4,517,222 articles. In a past comparison of encyclopedias, Wikipedia had almost double the amount of articles that Encyclopedia Britannica and Encarta had. The expansion of Wikipedia continues at a rapid pace due to the mass-collaboration of Wikipedians.
How do I get involved?
To edit articles on Wikipedia you do not have to log in. Just by clicking on the edit button located above each text allows you to get involved by contributing in any way that is comfortable, from checking grammar to restructuring an article, to doing code, or donating photographs. Visit the Contributing to Wikipedia page for more information. For important links and e-mail addresses see Wikipedia:Contact us.
Who writes the articles on Wikipedia?
Multiple individuals write and edit almost all articles on Wikipedia. If you click on the ‘View history’ tab at the top of an article, a list will be displayed of all the contributors to the article and when their contribution was made.
Who owns the encyclopedia articles?
By editing the articles on Wikipedia, many individuals have agreed to release their contributions under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike license. On these terms, the articles are free content and may be reproduced freely under this license. See Wikipedia:Copyrights and Wikipedia:Readers' FAQ for information on how you can use Wikipedia content. By law, the contributions are owned by the individuals who donated them. These people are not bound by the license and can use their property in the way they like. However, media with multiple authors require permission from every contributor to use them differently from the terms of the Wikipedia license.
Who is responsible for the articles on Wikipedia?
Including you, anyone is responsible for the articles. Contributing information to different parts of the project can be done by anyone. All you need is to know how to edit a page, and have some encyclopedic knowledge that you would like to share. You can learn who is responsible for the most recent edits of any given page by clicking on the ‘View history’ link. If you find an error in the latest revision of an article, you are encouraged correct it. This basic review mechanism maintains the reliability of the encyclopedia. As a result, Wikipedia has become one of the most extensive information libraries available on the Internet.
Can I rely on Wikipedia for advice on medical, legal & financial, safety and other critical issues?
No. If you need specific advice please seek a professional who is licensed or knowledgeable in that area. Valuable and accurate information in Wikipedia. However, Wikipedia cannot guarantee the validity of the information found. The content of any given article may recently have been changed, vandalized or altered by someone whose opinion does not correspond with the state of knowledge in the relevant fields. See Wikipedia:General disclaimer for more cautionary information.
Should I create an account? Can't I just edit articles anonymously?
Editing as an unregistered user is acceptable. However, without a user name you will not be able to create or rename pages. Some pages are protected from editing by unregistered users to prevent vandalism. Wikipedians with user names are more anonymous, while anyone can see the IP address of a user who did not log in when they edited. You may prefer to create a user name for yourself in order to hide your IP address, if you are concerned with privacy. Editors with user names enjoy several benefits. Among them is the positive reputation that goes with quality work and neutralizing article disputes.
How do you know if the information is correct?
It is possible for biased, out-of-date, or incorrect information to be posted as anyone can edit an article. There are many individuals monitoring contributions using the ‘Recent Changes’ page, incorrect information is, therefore, quickly corrected. You are encouraged to help by correcting articles, validating content, and providing useful references.
How do you prevent people from ruining articles? (Defacement or vandalism)
All changes to an article are registered in a ’page history’, so any vandalism can be replaced by an older version of the page. Recent changes to Wikipedia are automatically listed on a special page for that purpose. Software robots immediately reverse obvious defacement automatically. Any user can add a particular page to a personal ‘watchlist’, which shows when a page is updated and allows the user to check whether the update is a substantial contribution. To stop a recurrent problem, an article can be temporarily protected from editing. User names and IP addresses can also be blocked from editing.
What can I do about libelous content or an invasion of privacy?
Wikipedia is easy to edit so you can simply revert wrong or hurtful information yourself. However, because every revision is logged, special steps are required to remove this information from the historical record. Please see Wikipedia:Oversight and Wikipedia:Libel for Wikipedia's policy on removing historical revisions, and how to request such a change.
Which wiki software does Wikipedia run on?
Wikipedia and the other Wikimedia projects use the MediaWiki software to facilitate collaborative editing and storage of page histories.
