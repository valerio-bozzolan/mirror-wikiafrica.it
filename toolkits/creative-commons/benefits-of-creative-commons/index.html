More about Benefits of Creative Commons

Creative Commons helps you share your knowledge and creativity with the world. Creative Commons develops, supports, and stewards legal and technical infrastructure that maximizes digital creativity, sharing, and innovation.

Why use CC?

CC provides global recognition

Content creators in the digital realm are increasingly recognising the CC concept and iconography. Content sites are employing CC licensing in their upload process and are creating CC-specific search engines.

CC has an international application

Creative Commons’ licences are designed to be global in their application and enforceable in any jurisdiction. More than 50 countries around the world hold existing Jurisdiction-tailored licences. Generic licences also exist for countries that currently do not have jurisdiction-specific licences.

CC licences are easy to choose

Not all individuals are aware of copyright laws and how they work, which is why Creative Commons have made it quick and simple for content creators to choose how users reuse their content, while still preserving full copyright ownership of their work. By using the license generator on the international Creative Commons website, content creators, can easily identify the appropriate licence (http://www.creativecommons.org/license ). Available for download is the source code for the licence generator, allowing other websites to incorporate it into their own upload process. The photo sharing site, Flickr (https://www.flickr.com/creativecommons/) hosts a CC licence generator on its site.

CC licences are easy to understand

A licence button exists for each CC licence. Attaching the button to content notifies users that it is CC licensed and provides them with access to the ‘Licence Deed’ that simply defines the application of the content. If users require more information, the full legal code of the licence can be accessed via the licence button.

CC material is easy to find

The CC licence generator embeds licence-specific technical code into digital files. This code enables users to easily search for content available for reuse and/or for material that allows for particular uses (e.g. for commercial purposes). A number of search engines, content sites and programs currently allow people to search specifically for and/or organise CC-licensed material.

Why offer CC?

CC offers flexibility.

Creative Commons provides a wide range of licensing options, with licences ranging from non-commercial verbatim reuse only to unrestricted commercial use. In addition, using the CC licence generator, creators can be given the option to choose exactly how they want to make their material available, rather than imposing a one-size-fits-all terms of use agreement. Flickr takes this approach.

CC is legally sound

As with all ‘open’ licences, the CC licences are continuously drafted, redrafted and subject to on going discussion and analysis. A new set of lawyers review the legal code each time a new project jurisdiction begins the process of porting the licences. Additionally, a number of international court hearings have enforced CC licences, providing clear jurisprudence on their application. A significant body of academic discourse has also grown around the topic, potentially assisting with licensing interpretation.

CC is remix ready

Being a prior permissions system, Creative Commons allows for licences that permit derivative works, to be specifically designed for remixing. All CC licences are internally compatible, permitting creators to draw upon the millions of items currently available for remixing. CC also has a suite of sampling licences, which allow for reuse of small parts of an original work, but limit reuse of the entire work. Internationally, CC licenses are increasingly becoming the most widely recognised and established remixing licences available.

CC assists with internal rights management

Under a CC licence, you as a content aggregator are free to use the content under the same terms as its users. Ensuring that you comply with the license terms, this enables you to use CC content in various ways (for example, in your own films or for promotional activities). So as to avoid problems such as orphaned works and out-dated terms (that often arise with more ‘specific’ copyright licences), the licences are have also been devised to be sufficiently broad and technologically neutral.

CC reduces licence proliferation.

The use of standardised CC licences ensures the creators’ work is compatible with worldwide content that is also using CC, allowing for cross-content remixing to take place. Additionally, it saves you the time and expense of generating your own specific set of licences and in turn, will not require you to learn another licensing system.

CC offers integrity in its licensing model

Given that there are now so many CC organisations worldwide, the integrity of the CC licences is maintained by hosting on the international Creative Commons website (http://creativecommons.org). The embedded licence metadata also means that, if attached correctly, the licence information stays with the content regardless of where it is distributed.

CC supports community building & a culture of sharing

Using CC licences can be an effective way of encouraging the acts of sharing and reusing within an online community. As the licences set the default rules for the reuse of content on your site, they can be used as a fundamental tool for structuring relationships between the users of your site.

CC provides access to infrastructure & support materials

By using the CC system, you gain access to all the CC support tools designed to make the licensing system easier to use. These include the intuitive licence generator, dedicated CC search engines and licence badges. There are also a range of support documents and FAQs available on the main CC website (http://creativecommons.org).
